Levels of the Neck

The neck is divided into lymph node levels that help describe disease extent and guide neck dissection.

  1. Level I – submental and submandibular nodes bordered by the digastric muscles and mandible.
  2. Level II – upper jugular nodes from the skull base to the hyoid bone, anterior to the sternocleidomastoid.
  3. Level III – middle jugular nodes between the hyoid and cricoid cartilage.
  4. Level IV – lower jugular nodes from the cricoid cartilage to the clavicle.
  5. Level V – nodes of the posterior triangle along the spinal accessory nerve.
  6. Level VI – anterior compartment nodes surrounding the larynx and trachea.

Understanding these levels is essential for staging head and neck cancers.
